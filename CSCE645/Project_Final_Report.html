Title: Sweep volume generation of an Unmanned Ground Vehicle with dynamic geometry.

Name: Pradeep Garigipati

  • Problem Summary: The volume swept by a three dimensional object when moved along a path is known as the Swept Volume of the object with respect to a trajectory. Computation of an implicit representation of the volume swept by an object would help us in many ways. For example, it would help in identifying if the object would interfere with any other objects during its motion along a particular path. One of the apparent applications of this technique in the field of robotics is for efficient collision detection. The current problem being adressed is that of generating the swept volume of robot (Unmanned Ground Vehicle) shape for given path under the assumption that motion is planar. However, the object(robot) is allowed to rotate about the normal to plane of motion.

  • Previous work:

    • Martin, R R et.al. (1990) - The paper presents a theoretical basis for computation of swept volumes by solid objects in 3 dimensonal space using envelope theory from differential geometry.
    • Abdel-Malek K et.al. (2000) - The paper presents a practical approach on how to represent swept volume of implicit surfaces using Jacobian rank deficiency condition.
